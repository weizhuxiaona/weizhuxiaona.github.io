    Farm Hand

    Background

    Waggit is a mobile application to connect available dog owners with trusted dog walkers and helps manage the relationship between dog walkers and their clients.

    My Role: I was the user researcher, interaction designer and user experience designer, and maybe a bit of project manager (documented the deliverables) in this project.

    Collaborator: Ken Gard, Rachel Schirra, and Mark Rodriguez

    Design Process

    Design Question

    How can we help to connect urban dog owners with willing and available dog walkers?

    We redefined our design question to specify an urban local. Dog walking as a service is primarily offered in urban areas where people may or may not have the space to let their dog roam freely. Besides, we had a bunch of research questions to guide us the direction of primary and secondary research.

    User Research

    First of all, we considered our stakeholders and created the stakeholder map.Then, we used three methods to analyze users’ needs.

    1. User Interview: We interviewed three dog owners, and one dog walker. We found out that sometimes dog owners are too busy to walk their dogs while they often did not ask their friends from help, considering it is really a burden for others. They mostly use internet search or friends’ recommendation to find a dog walker, while they would be willing to use the dog walking service only if the features are satisfied. Moreover, especially as for the dog owners, they concern more about dog walkers’ trust, location, and experience.

    2. Affinity Diagrams: We created two affinity diagrams, one for dog walker and one for dog owner, to categorize their problems and needs.

    3. Competitive Product Analysis: There is no existing dog walker finder service, particularly no such service on mobile. However, it is obvious and also known from the research findings that people nowadays rely more on mobile phone, and it is better to provide a mobile dog walker finder service; There are existing services of GPS for dog walking, so that we can apply this technology to the service we want to design.

    Ideation

    Firstly, we each came up with ten ideas and sketched them. Then, through a hot discussion, we evaluated these ideas according to two basic principles – User Impact and Feasibility. Finally, we chose the best three ideas and re-sketched (by Rachel). At the same time, I created a simple functional structure to help with the prototype.

    Prototype

    Based on re-sketched ideas, we created paper prototype, and tested with three potential users, then refined the paper prototype (by Nina). We learned Justinmind by ourselves (by Ken, Nina, and Mark) and collaborated to make the hi-fi prototype (you can click and view the hi-fi prototype online). Though, the hi-fi prototype is not that perfect, but, we were really devoted ourselves and proud of the learning process and collaboration for only in a few days!
