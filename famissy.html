Famissy

Background

Famissy (Family+Miss) is a private sharing platform for the family, especially for the parents and children. It is like a digital family, recording all the beautiful memory of the family. Family member can share whatever (pictures, videos, status, location, and moods etc.) they want to the whole family without any disturbance. Also, this system will make efforts to encourage the parent-child communication in order to close them, improve their relationship (This project was for Web Product Design summer program, at Peking University, China).

My Role: I was the User Researcher and Experience Designer in this project. I took charge of the whole user research process: researched and wrote literature analysis report, conducted focus group and user interviews, created personas, assisted interaction and experience design, wrote part of the product design report, and presented our product in front of industry sponsors.

Collaborator: Zhuo Liu, Siyue Li, Yucan Wang, Bihong Cai, Chaoyu Yang

Design Process

Design Question

How can we help to improve the communication between parents and their child and tighten the parent-child relationship?

We had several hot discussions on what we wanted to do. Many cool ideas were raised up: weather/mood/calendar music app, travel peer connection service, micro expression app, toy exchange platform for kids, virtual shopping mall, family private social network, and gourmet map. Researched and evaluated each ideas’ advantages and disadvantages, we decided to work on the family private social network.

User Research

1. Literature Review: We investigated the parent-child communication in the Internet era, including the definition, characteristic, frequency, satisfaction, and barriers in this particular communication. Also, we discovered that many Internet-based needs and problems occurred these days. Like a double-edged sword, the Internet widens the communication gap between parents and child, while urged more convenient and direct communication channels for them.

2. User Interview: We acted as our users to conduct the focus group, and shared our own experience and needs to others. Based on the findings from focus group, we made fully preparations(interview questions, flow and requirements). Successfully interviewed three families with different communication features, we found out three parent-child communication types of families, according to which, we narrowed down and determined our target users: Students in middle school, high school and university who owns smartphone, and their parents, and both have desire to communicate with each other.

4. Competitive Product Analysis: We researched and analyzed similar products from several aspects — functionality for sharing information, effectiveness for facilitating communication, and usability for the operation of parents.

3. Personas: (1) highly frequency communication, highly desire, parents highly dependent on smartphone, specific needs of instantness and convenience (2) general frequency communication, highly desire, parents not that dependent on smartphone, specific needs of privacy (3) low frequency communication, general desire, parents less dependent on smartphone, specific needs of asynchronous sharing

Iterative Prototypes

It was really a hard time to make agreement on the functionalities, user flows and user interface design. We conducted several iterations (created simple lo-fi prototype and tested with users, and then discussed the iteration, and refined the prototype) and user testings, which were helpful. Finally, we focused on three subsystems -- "", specifically, simplify user flows and functionalities, let users concentrate on sharing, communication with each other.
