    Behavior Change


    Future Ball

    Design Question

    How can we motivate children (6-10) in urban areas to engage in the outdoors to promote physical and mental well-being and a long-term active lifestyle?

    Ideation Process

    Design Research

    We researched a lot of children's active lifestyles. The problem is really serious: In the past 30 years, childhood obesity has doubled in children. There are many effects of Obesity: Risk factors for Cardiovascular Disease; Prediabetes; Bone and Joint Problems; Sleep Apnea; Social and Psychological Problems; Increased Risk of Several Types of Cancer. What is worse, children are extremely lack of exercise, 88% of children reported using a computer every day while only 11% reported visiting a local park or nature area every day. While, Studies have shown there is benefit in the outdoors: increase positive mental health by 50%, and helps children develop physical and motor skills. Thus, we aimed to motivate children engage in the outdorrs to promote their physical and mental health.

    At the meantime, we also conducted competitive products analysis, to know about how existing technologies and products have been implemented to improve children's active lifestyle. One thing we found is the most effective -- Gamification, thus, we decided to utilize Gamification as well.

    Design Principles

    Enjoy Me

    There is nothing better than having fun outdoors without realizing you are getting a lot of healthy exercise and activity. In the game of Futureball, fun comes first and it never feels like a workout.

    Focus on the activity, not the technology

    To have fun outdoors, we need to become free of the technology that is present in every facet of life. Futureball lets kids have fun, hands-free.

    Interactivate

    We know play comes first, but Futureball stands out from the rest. There are no screens involved, and the only way to play is together. Interactive games with your friends and family make Futureball the happiest.
